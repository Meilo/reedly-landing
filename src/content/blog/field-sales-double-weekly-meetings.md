You just spent forty minutes driving to a prospect who forgot about the meeting. It's the second no-show this week. Meanwhile, your colleague in the next territory somehow manages to run eight qualified appointments per week while you're struggling to get four on the calendar.

The gap isn't talent. It's method.

## Block prospecting time like it's a client meeting

Most field reps prospect "when they have time." That's the problem. Top performers do the opposite: **they block 90 minutes every Tuesday and Thursday morning exclusively for booking appointments**. No exceptions. No "I'll do it later." During that window, the phone is off, the CRM is open, and the only job is to fill next week's calendar.

The result? A predictable pipeline of meetings instead of the feast-or-famine cycle that kills momentum.

## Use the 30-kilometer rule

Stop driving across the entire region for scattered appointments. High-performing reps divide their territory into zones and **schedule all meetings in the same area on the same day**. When you're meeting a prospect in the industrial park on the west side, you call every other target in that zone before you leave.

One rep I know went from 12 appointments per month to 23 just by clustering geographically. Same effort, double the face time.

## Ask for referrals while you're still in the room

The best moment to get your next meeting isn't next week. It's during the current one. Before you pack up, ask: *"Who else in your network deals with this same challenge?"* You'd be surprised how often clients will text a contact right there and introduce you.

Field prospecting efficiency isn't about working harder. It's about working in patterns that compound. Block the time. Cluster the geography. Ask before you leave. Do this consistently for three weeks and watch your calendar fill up naturally.
